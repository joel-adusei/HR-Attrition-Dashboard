# HR-Attrition-Dashboard

Project Overview

      This project analyses employee attrition patterns across departments, demographics, salary bands, job roles, and tenure metrics to uncover the key     drivers influencing workforce retention.

  Built using modern data visualisation techniques, the dashboard enables HR teams and business stakeholders to proactively address retention challenges, optimise workforce planning, and improve employee satisfaction.

Business Problem

  Employee attrition remains a major challenge for organisations, leading to:
  
  Increased recruitment and onboarding costs
  Productivity losses
  Knowledge gaps within teams
  Reduced employee morale
  Operational disruption

  Many organisations struggle to identify the root causes of attrition due to fragmented HR data and limited analytical visibility.
  
  This project addresses these challenges by consolidating employee data into an interactive HR analytics dashboard that highlights workforce trends and provides actionable insights to improve retention strategies.

Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this project are to:

  ✅ Consolidate employee and attrition data into a centralised dashboard
  
  ✅ Analyse workforce turnover trends and identify high-risk employee groups
  
  ✅ Provide actionable HR insights to support strategic decision-making
  
  ✅ Improve employee retention through data-driven recommendations
  
  ✅ Enhance workforce planning and organisational stability

Key Insights
🔹 High Departmental Attrition

Research & Development and Sales recorded the highest employee turnover levels, indicating potential challenges around workload, employee engagement, and career progression.

🔹 Early Career Turnover

Employees with 0–2 years of service showed the highest attrition rates, suggesting opportunities to improve onboarding and employee integration processes.

🔹 Compensation Impact

Most attrition occurred within lower salary bands, highlighting the importance of competitive compensation and employee benefits.

🔹 Younger Workforce Trends

Employees aged 18–30 accounted for the largest proportion of turnover, reflecting changing workforce expectations around flexibility, wellbeing, and career development.
